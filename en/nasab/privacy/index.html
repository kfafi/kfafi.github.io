Privacy Policy — Nasab

Last updated 23 June 2026

Nasab is made by Kefafi (“we”, “us”). Nasab is a private family-tree app for Arab families. It is not a social network: there is no public feed, no discovery, and no shared directory. This policy explains what we collect and how we use and protect it.

So you can reach your tree from your devices and share it with people you choose, Nasab stores your data on a secure server with our service provider, under your control. The core principle: you own your tree, you control who sees it, and you can revoke access at any time.

What we collect

  • Your account: your email address and password (stored hashed — we never see it), and an optional display name and preferred language.
  • About email: the app does not verify that you own the email address. Use an email you control — it is how you reset your password and receive invitations.
  • The tree content you enter: relatives' names (Arabic and Latin), gender, relationships and marriages, birth/death dates (Hijri and Gregorian), places, biographies, photo URLs you provide (we store the link you enter, not uploaded image files), tribe affiliations, and per-record privacy settings.
  • Sharing data: the email addresses you invite and the roles you grant them.
  • Technical logs: standard server logs (IP address, timestamps) kept by our infrastructure provider.

How we use it

We use it only to run the app: to sign you in, store and display your tree, enforce the privacy and sharing settings you choose, and keep the service secure. Nothing else.

What we don't do

  • No advertising.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking.
  • We never sell your data.

Where your data is stored, and security

Your data is stored in a Supabase (Postgres) database with Row-Level Security and is private by default. Living people are private by default. Data travels over encrypted connections (HTTPS), and passwords are stored hashed.

Sharing and privacy

Sharing is yours to control, per tree: you invite someone with a link and grant a viewer (read) or editor role, and you can revoke access at any time. Every record has its own visibility, and living people are private by default.

Data about other people

By its nature, Nasab records family members, who may include minors. You are responsible for what you enter and must have the right or consent to record other people's information. Defaults protect the living, and anyone may contact us to review or remove their data.

Your rights

  • Access and correction: view and edit your data in the app.
  • Export: export your tree as GEDCOM.
  • Deletion: delete your account and all your data — see Account deletion.

Account and data deletion

You can delete your account from inside the app at any time: menu → “Delete account” → confirm. This deletes your account, every tree you own and all the people and marriages in them, and all sharing grants to and from your account — immediately and permanently. Tribes you created remain as shared reference for others. If you cannot sign in, email support@kefafi.dev and we will do it for you. Full details on the support page.

Service providers

We rely on Supabase for hosting, authentication, and the database; your data is stored on its managed infrastructure, governed by Supabase's Privacy Policy. Nasab integrates no advertising, analytics, or tracking provider.

Children

The app is not directed at children as users. A tree may include minors entered by its owner; the living-person privacy defaults protect them. To request removal of a minor's data, contact us.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the date above and post the new version at the same address.
